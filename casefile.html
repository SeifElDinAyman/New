Section 01

Initial Report

Approximately one year ago, a man — later registered as S.E.D — was found wandering alone near the Maadi district. He carried no identification. He bore no visible wounds. He was covered in blood.

The subject was in shock. Trembling hands. Bloodshot eyes. Nicotine consumption far exceeding any normal behavioral threshold. He repeated only one statement:

"They took it… …they took it."

Before further questioning could proceed — he disappeared. No records. No trace. The case was closed.

Until now.

Section 02

Recent Developments

The same individual has resurfaced — voluntarily, or so he claims. Calm. Controlled. He speaks deliberately. He chooses his words the way a man chooses his weapons.

No longer in visible distress — composed to an unusual degree
Signs of selective memory recovery — some details now clear, others still absent
An engagement ring observed on his right hand
Refuses to name the suspect — states it is not yet time
"Because she still doesn't know what she did. And I think it's time she found out."

Internal Note — Restricted:

He claims the abduction left him unconscious throughout — yet he surfaces with fragments. Flashbacks. Details that contradict each other in ways only a conscious witness could produce.

Our analysts have reached one conclusion: this man is not seeking justice. He is preparing for revenge.

He came to us voluntarily — or so he claims. In truth, he was performing. He needed an official case number. He needed a detective assigned. And then — he intended to work entirely behind our backs. We cannot allow that. But we also cannot ignore what he knows.

Section 03

Medical & Psychological Notes

Persistent low-grade tremors in both hands — origin undetermined
Symptoms consistent with prolonged nicotine withdrawal
Elevated emotional response under specific, unidentified triggers
Subject smiles when describing the incident — cannot explain why

The subject continues to report a wound at the center of his chest. He places his hand over it instinctively when speaking about the case. We have examined him three times. No incision. No bruising. No visible trauma.

And yet he insists — with complete conviction — that something was removed from that location. He is not here to get it back. He is here to make sure the person who took it understands what she is holding.

Section 05 — Restricted Testimony

Victim Flashback Log

The subject claims to have been sedated throughout the abduction. What follows is a sequence of consciousness windows — partial fragments recovered through repeated psychological interview. Each is an active investigative lead.

Flashback 01 — Entry Point
Brought through a door bearing a number. The number itself has not been recovered. He is certain it existed.
▸ Numbered entry — possible unit, office, or floor. Cross-reference Maadi building layouts.
Flashback 02 — Setting
The space was office-like. Structured. Enclosed. Not a home — not a public space. He associates it with formality and contained control.
▸ Private office or commercial unit. Suspect has access to a non-residential interior.
Flashback 03 — Sensory
The air carried a persistent tropical scent — sweet, unfamiliar. Corroborates Evidence #2. He has reported this smell involuntarily since.
▸ Air freshener, perfume, or product in use at the site. Possible brand identification.
Flashback 04 — Recording
During a window of awareness, he was being questioned. A video of him was being recorded. He does not recall the content of the questions.
▸ If the location is confirmed, CCTV retrieval may be possible — but cannot be actioned without establishing the site first.
Flashback 05 — Broadcast
A television was running in the background. He did not register the content clearly — but a programme was airing at the time.
▸ High-value lead: identifying the broadcast allows airtime logs to be cross-referenced for an accurate crime timestamp.
Flashback 06 — Codename
The suspect was communicating via WhatsApp and insisted on being addressed by a codename — even in that channel. Exact phrase recovered:
"Even on WhatsApp use my codename."
▸ Suspect maintains a concealed identity even in private digital communication. Codename not yet recovered.
Flashback 07 — Food Order
The suspect ordered food during the detention. The victim has a partial sensory impression — but cannot confirm the order or the source.
▸ Delivery records from takeaway services in the Maadi radius may yield timestamp and location data if the order can be reconstructed.
Flashback 08 — Audiobook & Password
The suspect was listening to an audiobook and laughed — then remarked that the name just mentioned was the password to their computer. The suspect appeared unconcerned, as though recovery of the password was already planned for.
▸ The book can potentially be identified. The password is a character name at a specific point in a specific recording. Find the book — find the password.
Flashback 09 — Physical Feature
The victim is certain the suspect possesses a distinctive physical characteristic. It registered even through sedation-impaired perception. He cannot yet name it.
▸ Status: suppressed — not yet recovered. May surface through further triggered recall.
Flashback 10 — Alias & Theft
In the final window, the suspect stated their alias and declared it was their real name. During this same moment — something personal belonging to the victim was taken.
▸ This flashback directly implicates the theft in the core complaint. The alias is the primary unresolved identifier in this investigation.
